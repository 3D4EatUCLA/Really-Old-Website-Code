3D4E at UCLA

ABOUT US

3D4E (3D For Everyone) is an intercollegiate network for enthusiasts of 3D printing and rapid-prototyping. Chapters span across the southwest, including UCLA, University of Southern California, San Diego State University, University of Arizona, and many others, and membership is constantly expanding.

Our clubâ€™s main focus is on group projects, in which we design, manufacture, and test various objects using CAD software and 3D printing. These projects range from purely fun, like RC cars, to life-changing, like our prosthetic hand. 3D4E also provides the support, resources, and expertise for members to pursue their individual projects.

3D4E at UCLA is sponsored by AIO Robotics, a Los Angeles-based printer company that generously provides us with the printers and filament necessary to pursue our hands-on design projects. Space for storage and operation of our printers is furnished by the UCLA Library system. We are also sponsored by Blackstone LaunchPad, a start-up space that supports club events.

PROJECTS

3D4E Projects

Every year, 3D4E works on a series of club projects. Most recently, 3D4E at UCLA designed and 3D Printed a robot to compete against the other 3D4E chapters. In Fall 2015, 3D4E will launch new projects, including designing 3D Printed Prosthetics. Attend one of our meetings to find out more!

Club Partnerships

In addition to our own club projects, we have worked with many different organizations on campus. We have manufactured lightweight mounts for the UCLA Supermileage Vehicle Team, parts for UCLA IEEE Natcar competition vehicles, and a model cube satellite for UCLA Elfin. Please contact us to learn how we can work together.

Independent Projects

3D4E resources are also available for your independent projects. We can help you design and 3D Print your ideas. Contact us! We look forward to working with you!

  • Remote Vehicle


    In 2014, we 3D printed a remote control vehicle (everything except mechanical fasteners and springs were 3D printed) by connecting high-torque stepper motor with an Arduino board communicating through Bluetooth with an XBee controller.

  • RepRap 3D Printer


    The RepRap project asks a very simple question: "Can you print another 3D printer using a 3D printer?" The answer is "Yes!". This year, 3D4E at UCLA will be expanding our club resources by building a new 3D printer using open source software.

  • Klann's Mechanism Spider


    The Klann's linkage mechanism creates a bipedal-like motion from circular motions. The basic concept is easy to understand, but difficult to 3D print and implement.

  • Open Hand Project


    The open hand project is a partnership with UCLA Women's Basketball Team to modify Enabling the Future's prosthetic hand model to be able to play sports.

  • SPY Volunteer Outreach


    3D4E is about sharing our knowledge and enthusiasm with everyone. This year, we are partnering with SPY (Safe Place for Youth) to help mentor young children about becoming a tinkering engineer.

  • Mouse Controller


    This year, 3D4E at UCLA will attempt to design and 3D print a working computer mouse. Not only will the mouse be fully functional, but each person can customize the size and shape to find the most ergonomic fit.
