3D4E at UCLA

ABOUT US

3D4E (3D For Everyone) is an intercollegiate network for enthusiasts of 3D printing and rapid-prototyping. There are currently four chapters in the network, founded by students at UCLA, University of Southern California, Loyola Marymount University, and San Diego State University.

In Fall 2014, 3D4E at UCLA co-hosted a 3D printing exhibition with Young's Research Library to demonstrate the capabilities of 3D printers. The event attracted the interest of more than 200 people and greatly increased the awareness of 3D printing on campus. In Spring 2015, we secured a sponsorship agreement with 3D printer manufacturer XYZPrinting Inc. Other partners include StartUP UCLA, a hub on campus for up and coming technologies and businesses, and the Blackstone LaunchPad, where we house our 3D printers and host workshops and events.

PROJECTS

3D4E Projects

Every year, 3D4E works on a series of club projects. Most recently, 3D4E at UCLA designed and 3D Printed a robot to compete against the other 3D4E chapters. In Fall 2015, 3D4E will launch new projects, including designing 3D Printed Prosthetics. Attend one of our meetings to find out more!

Club Partnerships

In addition to our own club projects, we have worked with many different organizations on campus. We have manufactured lightweight mounts for the UCLA Supermileage Vehicle Team, parts for UCLA IEEE Natcar competition vehicles, and a model cube satellite for UCLA Elfin. Please contact us to learn how we can work together.

Independent Projects

3D4E resources are also available for your independent projects. We can help you design and 3D Print your ideas. Contact us! We look forward to working with you!

  • Remote Vehicle


    In 2014, we 3D printed a remote control vehicle (everything except mechanical fasteners and springs were 3D printed) by connecting high-torque stepper motor with an Arduino board communicating through Bluetooth with an XBee controller.

  • RepRap 3D Printer


    The RepRap project asks a very simple question: "Can you print another 3D printer using a 3D printer?" The answer is "Yes!". This year, 3D4E at UCLA will be expanding our club resources by building a new 3D printer using open source software.

  • Klann's Mechanism Spider


    The Klann's linkage mechanism creates a bipedal-like motion from circular motions. The basic concept is easy to understand, but difficult to 3D print and implement.

  • Open Hand Project


    The open hand project is a partnership with UCLA Women's Basketball Team to modify Enabling the Future's prosthetic hand model to be able to play sports.

  • SPY Volunteer Outreach


    3D4E is about sharing our knowledge and enthusiasm with everyone. This year, we are partnering with SPY (Safe Place for Youth) to help mentor young children about becoming a tinkering engineer.

  • Mouse Controller


    This year, 3D4E at UCLA will attempt to design and 3D print a working computer mouse. Not only will the mouse be fully functional, but each person can customize the size and shape to find the most ergonomic fit.

FAQ

Q: Do you need any prior experience with anything to join the club?

A: No, you are not required to know anything. We will give you guidance on what to learn and help you with any question. We also host various kinds of workshops throughout the school year so you can hop in any time even if you're not a member of the club.


Q: What resources do I have access to if I don't pay the member fee?

A: You are welcomed to come to our workshops throughout the year and join our projects. You can print out project related things with our printer.


Q: What extra resources do I have access to as a member? (If I pay the annual member fee of $15)

A: You get unlimited prints to one of our printers. You also get a member T-shirt designed by our wonderfully artistic secretary Rebecca.


Q: Can I participate in more than one project?

A: You are welcomed to join both projects though we highly recommended you plan your time wisely before doing so.


Q: What if I'm interested in 3D printing but not in any of the club projects?

A: You are certainly still welcomed to come to our meetings to know the latest announcements and project developments. Otherwise you can come to our workshops and events. We can also still answer any question you have about 3D printing or our club. Or you can pay a member fee and use our printers to study 3D printing.
