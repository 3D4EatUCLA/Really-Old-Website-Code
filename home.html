ABOUT US

3D4E (3D For Everyone) is an intercollegiate network for enthusiasts of 3D printing and rapid-prototyping. There are currently four chapters in the network, founded by students at UCLA, University of Southern California, Loyola Marymount University, and San Diego State University.

In Fall 2014, 3D4E at UCLA co-hosted a 3D printing exhibition with Young's Research Library to demonstrate the capabilities of 3D printers. The event attracted the interest of more than 200 people and greatly increased the awareness of 3D printing on campus. In Spring 2015, we secured a sponsorship agreement with 3D printer manufacturer XYZPrinting Inc. Other partners include StartUP UCLA, a hub on campus for up and coming technologies and businesses, and the Blackstone LaunchPad, where we house our 3D printers and host workshops and events.

EXECUTIVE BOARD

HO MING YIM

President

As president of 3D4E at UCLA, Ho Ming works closely with the executive board and looks over all members in the club. Majoring in Chemical Engineering and minoring is Geospatial Information System & Technology, Ho Ming has a very broad set of skills from computer aided design to website design, geospatial analysis to numerical analysis, and process flow modeling to computer modeling.

MERRICK CAMPBELL

Vice President

Merrick Campbell is currently an senior undergraduate mechanical engineering student at UCLA. He co-founded 3D4E at UCLA to help other students explore the boundary between engineering and multi-media through 3D Printing. Outside of lab and studies, he enjoys reading, cooking, and constructing open-source 3D Printers. As an Eagle Scout, he mentors current scouts through the Bruin Scout program at UCLA.
