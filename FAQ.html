3D4E at UCLA - FAQs


This section contains a wealth of information, related to 3D4E at UCLA and its operation. If you cannot find an answer to your question, make sure to contact us through our Facebook Page.

General questions

What is 3D4E?

3D4E (3D For Everyone) is an intercollegiate network of university clubs who focuses on using 3D printing to pursue hands-on technical project.

How do I join 3D4E at UCLA?

3D4E at UCLA offers two types of membership: free membership and paid membership.
  • Free membership gives you the opportunity to get hands on experience, participate in club projects, and attend 3D4E related events. Check our website for the calendar for upcoming club events, and the project sign up link to sign up for projects.
  • Paid membership gives you access to our club printers (stored in Blackstone LaunchPad in Covel Commons), a complimentary T-Shirt, and mentorship on personal projects.

Do I need prior experience to join the club/participate in projects?

No, you are not required to know anything. We will give you guidance on what to learn and help you with any question. We also host various kinds of workshops throughout the school year so you can hop in any time even if you're not a member of the club.

What is the time commitment for the club?

There is no minimum time requirement for the club. You can dedicate as many hours as you'd like per week to the club, but we suggest setting aside two to three hours per week so you can stay on top of club activities and updates. Attendance at general meetings is not required. If you cannot make it to meetings, but would like to be in a project, you can report your progress via email.

Will CAD (computer-aided-design) and 3D printing training be provided?

Yes. We generally do a large workshop at the beginning of the academic year. We can also arrange training sessions for CAD & 3D Printing on an ad hoc basis. If there is enough interests from new members, we can arrange larger group training sessions.

How I access club's printer for personal projects?

To access our printer you have to have paid membership dues and trained on how to properly use a 3D printer. You can contact us to arrange a training workshop.

Can I participate in more than one project?

You are welcomed to join both projects though we highly recommended you plan your time wisely before doing so.

What if I'm interested in 3D printing but not in any of the projects?

You are certainly still welcomed to come to our meetings to know the latest announcements and project developments. Otherwise you can come to our workshops and events. We can also still answer any question you have about 3D printing or our club. Or you can pay a member fee and use our printers to study 3D printing.

SPY Volunteering

What is SPY?

SPY, or Safe Place for Youth, is a homeless youth shelter in Venice Beach that provides a variety of services, from basics like food and medical care to long term solutions like helping the youth obtain stable housing.

What do you plan to teach the youth?

Our volunteers will be educating the youth about CAD modeling as well as how to use 3D printers.

How much of a time commitment can volunteers expect?

This will vary as our program expands, but for now since we are keeping a small group of volunteers as a sort of trial run, volunteers can expect to spend an average of 3 hours once every few weeks.

How can I get involved in this project?

Get in touch with us by emailing 3D4E.ucla@gmail.com! If you are already a club member, you can contact the project lead for our SPY outreach project, Brooke Zampell.

Why did 3D4E at UCLA choose to work with homeless youth?

Homeless youth are one of the most underserved populations. Since they are often underaged, they have difficulty securing jobs, and do not have nearly as many resources available to them as homeless adults. We chose to serve these youth since it is a great opportunity for us to effectively utilize our resources and provide the youth with a future career opportunity.
